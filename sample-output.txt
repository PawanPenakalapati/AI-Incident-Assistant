Incident Summary
Production login failures due to system slowness; services restored via restart; root cause remains inconclusive.

Business Impact
Users were unable to access the application, impacting critical operations and causing service disruption.
Increased risk of SLA breach due to repeated occurrence and delayed root cause identification.

Observations
High DB connections indicate possible resource saturation or connection leak.
Incomplete logs limited accurate root cause validation.
Temporary fixes (restart, cache clear) restored service but did not address underlying issue.
Repeat occurrence suggests unresolved systemic problem.
Multiple contributing factors observed across DB and application layers.

Corrective Actions (Immediate)
High | Owner: Application Team → Validate system stability post-restart over defined monitoring window.
High | Owner: DB Team → Analyze DB connections and identify abnormal spikes or leaks.
Medium | Owner: Infra Team → Capture and preserve all relevant logs for RCA.

Preventive Actions (Short-term)
High | Owner: Incident Manager → Conduct cross-team RCA covering DB, application, and infrastructure layers.
Medium | Owner: DB Team → Implement monitoring and alerts for DB connection thresholds.
Medium | Owner: Infra Team → Improve logging completeness and retention mechanisms.
Low | Owner: Application Team → Review cache and session handling mechanisms.

RACI Matrix
Responsible (R): Application Team, DB Team, Infra Team
Accountable (A): Incident Manager / IT Operations Lead
Consulted (C): Vendor Support, Security Team
Informed (I): Business Stakeholders, Delivery Leadership

Root Cause Note
Root cause is inconclusive. Indicators suggest multiple potential contributing factors, including DB connection saturation and application behavior. Further coordinated analysis required.
